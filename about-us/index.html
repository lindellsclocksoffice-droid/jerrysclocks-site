Our Story

About Jerry Lindell

A Craftsman's Legacy

Six Decades of Clockmaking Mastery

Jerry Lindell is a 1964 graduate of the Baronian School of Clock and Watch Making in Philadelphia — one of the most prestigious institutions in American horology. As a second-generation clockmaker, his expertise is not just learned, it's inherited.

What truly sets Lindell's apart is simple: every single repair is handled personally by Jerry from start to finish. A dedicated team takes care of scheduling, customer service, and the day-to-day so Jerry can focus on what he does best — the clocks themselves.

Jerry has built a complete grandfather clock movement entirely by hand — including fabricating the tools needed to make certain parts. That level of mastery means there is no clock he cannot repair.

  • 1964 graduate of the Baronian School of Clock & Watch Making, Philadelphia
  • Second-generation horologist — the craft passed down through family
  • Over 33 years serving Florida, Georgia, the Carolinas, Virginia, Maryland, Delaware & Philadelphia
  • Fully equipped on-site shop with lathes, mills, and precision tools
  • Specializes in antique movements with no commercially available parts
"Traditional skills combined with superior technique and expertise — that's what separates us from the rest. Most of our business comes from referrals from satisfied customers."— Jerry Lindell, Proprietor
